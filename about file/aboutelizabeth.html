ELIZABETH TAYLOR

Elizabeth Taylor was born on February 27, 1932, in London, England, to American parents. Her family moved to the United States before World War II, and she grew up in Los Angeles. She started acting at a young age and quickly became a child star.

Her big break came at age 12 with the movie National Velvet (1944), where she played a girl who trains a horse to race. The film was a huge success, and Elizabeth became one of the most famous young actresses in Hollywood.

As she grew older, she transitioned into adult roles and became known not just for her beauty, but for her powerful performances. She starred in major films like Giant, Cat on a Hot Tin Roof, and Butterfield 8, which earned her an Academy Award.

In 1963, she played the title role in Cleopatra, one of the most expensive movies ever made at the time. The film was famous not only for its scale, but also for her romance with co-star Richard Burton. Their relationship became world-famous.
